The original draft of this post was written almost a year ago, as I was *actually* going through the graduate application process. In retrospect, maybe this is better. 

As anyone would tell you, the graduate school process is *hard*. It starts with years of research and extracurricular work (if you know to be strategic...). The 8 months between August (when applications open) and April (when you commit to graduate school) seem to be the longest months of your life. But to me, the hardest part was the last few weeks: once you've collected your offers, finished all your visits, and now sit before your admissions portal, choosing where to spend the next half decade of your life. 

No one tells you that this choice feels fundamentally different than choosing your undergraduate. This time, you're an adult.

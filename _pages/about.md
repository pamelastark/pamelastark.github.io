About Me
======

Hi there! My name is Pam Stark, and I am a Physics Ph.D. student and [Knight-Hennessy Scholar](https://knight-hennessy.stanford.edu/people/pam-stark) at Stanford University. I earned my S.B. in Physics from MIT with a concentration in computation.

My Ph.D. research focuses on developing quantum sensing techniques to improve particle searches for axion dark matter. I split my time between several major axion experiments at Stanford (DM-Radio, ADMX-Vera) and R&D efforts at the Stanford National Accelerator Center (SLAC). In the past, I worked on the [CUORE](https://cuore.lngs.infn.it) experiment searching for neutrinoless double beta decay and led the detector's first high-multiplicity search for fractionally-charged particles. 

Outside of my research, I am interested in science outreach, outreach, policy, and equity & inclusion efforts. In addition to being a SLAC tour guide, a regular KIPAC outreach volunteer, and member of the SLAC Users Organization, I am involved in physics education efforts as a Leadership in Inclusive Teaching Fellow at Stanford. 

On this website, you can find information about my research interests, random thoughts about science, and my perspectives on equity and inclusion within academic physics. For further inquiries, please reach out to me by email (through my sidebar) or follow me on Twitter :)
